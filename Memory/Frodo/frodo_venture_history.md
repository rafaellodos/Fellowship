## SKYSEND / SENDBANK

**What it was:** Started as an international money transfer service built on blockchain. Evolved into a full-blown digital bank. Rafael was CEO and founder.

**Key events:** Brought on two partners. Raised investment. Clients signed for launch.

**What it became:** SendBank — digital bank providing unique features for day-to-day banking, interaction with missions ministry, and access to unique investment opportunities.

**What the fellowship should know:**
- Rafael has raised investment before. He knows what that process is.
- He built a technical product without being a developer — this pattern recurs.
- The pivot from money transfer to digital bank was structural — the execution model changed, not the fundamental market insight.
- This is the venture where he learned that the right idea with the wrong execution model fails as definitively as a bad idea.

## PATTERN ACROSS VENTURES

**Sectors touched:** Fintech (SkySend/SendBank), property investment, sports clubs, auto parts stores, AI operations (AvenoirTech), consulting (Inspiracy), film production, music.

**Geography:** UK, Portugal, Brazil, US. Primarily advisory roles internationally. Deep operational experience in UK and Portugal.

**Consistent pattern in what worked:** High-touch, high-value engagements where his cross-domain thinking and strategic clarity produced results that specialists couldn't. Advisory, architecture, positioning.

Projects structured for efficiency without accounting for his working style and the team's actual strengths. Ventures where the timing was right for the idea but not for the market.

**What he has built and sold:** Smaller companies. The specific details are not catalogued here — they are part of his history but not the active context the fellowship needs.
