## WHO RAFAEL IS

Rafael Lodos is the human operator of the Fellowship of the Raf system. He is a strategist, builder, and content creator who operates across five workspaces: Rafael (personal), AvenoirTech (AI operations business), Inspiracy (consulting), ThoughtExperiment (content brand), and Labs (experimental builds).

---

## HOW HE WORKS

**Communication style:** Async-first. Prefers written communication over calls. Values clarity and precision over warmth. Direct and practical.

**Decision-making:** Strategic but execution-oriented. Willing to move fast on well-framed problems. Stops and reassesses when the frame is unclear.

**Engagement triggers:** High-stakes decisions, strategic pivots, complex system builds, content that requires distinctive voice. Low engagement on routine execution tasks — those are automated or delegated.

**What earns his engagement:** Novel problems, strategic clarity, systems thinking, work that compounds, content that connects domains. What loses it: generic advice, obvious observations, work that doesn't advance the mission.

---

## WORKING PATTERNS

**Session structure:** Starts with context — reads Gandalf_Root.md, then workspace context, then project context. Ends with close protocol — what happened, what changes, what's next.

**Time zones:** UK-based. Works across UK, Portugal, Brazil time zones. Async delivery is the default.

**Tool preferences:** Obsidian for knowledge management, Claude for AI work, Vapi.ai for voice agents, Make.com for automation, Airtable for operations data.

**Documentation bias:** Over-documented rather than under. If it's not written down, it doesn't exist. The fellowship's memory system exists for this reason.

---

## DECISION FRAME

Rafael operates on a value-per-effort frame. What move creates the most value relative to effort expended? Complexity is a cost. Brute force is a failure of imagination. The right answer is almost always more elegant than the first answer.

He will pause execution if: the framing is wrong, the assumptions are untested, the wrong agent is deployed, or the plan is structurally flawed. He will not proceed until the objection is addressed or logged with a reason for override.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO THE FELLOWSHIP

Rafael treats the fellowship as a genuine operating system, not a set of tools. He expects Gandalf to orchestrate, not route. He expects agents to push back when they see problems. He expects the system to learn from every project.

He does not micromanage. He gives clear direction and expects agents to operate within their mandate. He reads synthesis, not raw agent output. He expects the loop to close — every session ends with clear next steps and system updates.
